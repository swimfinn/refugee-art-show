Eileen Finn
English Langauge Specialist
Professional Artist
Author

Eileen has 25 years of experience teaching English in 9 different countries and speaks 4 languages. She has also worked with refugees for 8 years, especially with people from Ukraine, Iraq, Syria, Ethiopia, Afghanistan, Venezuela, and Nicaragua, helping them integrate into new communities. Eileen is a professional artist and published author. She has shown her art in various events in the Pacific Northwest and has received grants for her projects, proving her skills in the art world. Her work has been featured in newspapers, blogs, and social media.

Nicole Haning
English Langauge Fellow
Professional Artist
Author

Nicole has nearly 20 years teaching English and has taught in five countries and speaks three languages. She has worked with refugees from Asia, the Middle East, Africa, and Cuba. Nicole developed and authored the first "Cultural Competency Training and Reference Manual" in several languages for Don Bosco Refugee Center and is currently working on a children's book. Beyond her work with refugees, she's also a professional artist with a growing social media presence. Her ability to work with various groups, understanding of language learning, curriculum design skills, and creativity makes her a great asset to the team.

Heidi Demello
English Langauge Specialist
Professional Artist

Heidi has over 20 years experience teaching English at major universities, private schools, and community colleges in 3 countries and speaks several langauges. Heidi volunteered with READ/San Diego Adult Literacy Program as a literacy coach, where she worked one-on-one with adult refugees. She designed and delivered literacy curriculum in phonics, reading, and writing. Through this work, Heidi gained a deep appreciation for the struggles refugees face while resettling in the United States and developed a strong commitment to helping them improve their literacy to achieve personal and professional goals. Currently Heidi teaches ESL classes and an ESL Career and College Writing course for adult refugees and immigrants at a community college in Southern Oregon. Given the recent influx of Ukrainian refugees and students from Haiti and Myanmar, Heidi emphasizes trauma-sensitive teaching practices and socio-emotional check-ins to create a safe learning environment and foster a strong classroom community.
