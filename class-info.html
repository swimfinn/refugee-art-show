Curriculum

# Date Time Teacher Class Title Course Description
1 May 20th Monday at 6:00 p.m. PST Nicole Haning What is art and the gallery process? In this class, you'll learn what art is and how it’s shown in galleries. We'll talk about what is considered art, the process of a gallery art show, and the elements of a gallery.By the end of this class, you'll know more about what art means and how the gallery process works. This is a great class if you're curious about art or thinking of a career in the art world.
2 May 22nd Wednesday at 6:00 p.m. PST Heidi Demello Art Therapy: Healing Through Creativity In this class, you'll explore how art can be a powerful tool for healing and emotional growth. We’ll discuss what art therapy is and how creating art can help reduce stress, improve mood, and offer a way to express feelings that are hard to put into words. You'll learn different art techniques that therapists use to help people manage anxiety, trauma, or depression. By the end of the class, you'll understand how art can support mental health and healing.
3 May 27th Monday at 6:00 p.m. PST Heidi Demello Art Career Essentials: Resume and Artist Statement This class is all about helping artists create a standout resume and a compelling artist statement. You'll learn how to showcase your skills, experience, and creativity in a way that grabs attention. The class will guide you in writing a professional resume that highlights your achievements and projects. You'll also craft an artist statement that tells your story and explains your artistic vision. By the end of the class, you'll have the tools to present yourself confidently to galleries, collectors, and art organizations.
4 May 29th Wednesday at 6:00 p.m. PST Nicole Haning Rock Social Media with Canva This class is for artists who want to use Canva and social media smarts to get their art seen! We'll show you how to create awesome posts for Instagram, Facebook, and other platforms. Learn to design eye-catching pictures and videos of your art, write captions that tell your story, and use the right hashtags to get discovered by new fans. We'll cover free features in Canva and some cool tricks (like stories and reels!) to make your content pop. By the end, you'll be a social media whiz and connect with a whole new audience who loves your art!
5 June 3rd Monday at 6:00 p.m. PST Eileen Finn Funding Your Project: The Art of Grant Writing & the Nuts & Bolts of Sales In this class, you'll learn how to write successful grant proposals for art projects. We'll cover the key steps, like researching grants, understanding requirements, and crafting a clear proposal. You'll discover how to explain your art project and its impact, and create a budget that makes sense. By the end of the class, you'll know how to find the right grants and increase your chances of getting funding for your art. Whether you're a beginner or have some experience, this class will help you take your art projects to the next level with grant support.
Then get ready to go over all the basics about getting gallery ready or selling your art. We'll go over questions regarding shipping, pricing, prints, distribution, etc., so that you can start to sell your art right away.
6 June 5th Wednesday at 6:00 p.m. PST Eileen Finn Self-Publish Like a Pro This class will guide you through the process of publishing your own book. You'll learn about the different steps involved, from editing and layout to cover design and printing options. We'll discuss how to choose the right platforms for self-publishing, like Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing or IngramSpark, and how to market your book to reach more readers. By the end of the class, you'll know how to create, publish, and promote your book independently. Whether you're writing fiction, non-fiction, or a collection of art or photography, this class will give you the tools to get your book out into the world.
